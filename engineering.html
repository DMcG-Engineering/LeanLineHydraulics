Capabilities

CNC Machining for Prototypes and Production

LeanLine Engineering works with overseas CNC partners to supply machined parts while keeping quoting, design conversations and support local in NSW.

CNC Turning

Precision-turned components for hydraulic, pneumatic and general mechanical applications.

  • Shafts, spigots, spacers and bushings
  • Threaded adapters and custom fittings
  • Grooves, O-ring lands and shoulders
  • Suitable for low to medium production runs

CNC Milling

Milled parts and plates for brackets, manifolds, mounting blocks and custom hardware.

  • Drilled and tapped hole patterns
  • Pockets, slots and counterbores
  • Simple 3D contours and features
  • Ideal for jigs, fixtures and small assemblies

Assembly-Friendly Design

Parts are reviewed from an engineering perspective to help ensure they’re practical to manufacture, assemble and use.

  • Advice on tolerances and fits
  • Suggestions to simplify machining
  • Focus on repeatability and robustness
  • Suited to real-world workshop conditions
Materials & Finishes

Materials, Anodising, Passivating & More

Parts can be supplied raw or with common surface treatments applied through our overseas machining and finishing partners, depending on what the application requires.

Common Materials

A selection of metals suited to hydraulic, pneumatic and general mechanical components.

  • Aluminium – ideal for lightweight components and manifolds
  • Mild steel – for brackets, flanges and structural parts
  • Stainless steel – for corrosion-resistant fittings and hardware
  • Engineering plastics (by request) – for guides, wear pads and covers

Surface Treatments

Optional treatments can be included in your quote so parts arrive ready for use or final assembly.

  • Anodising (including colour options) for aluminium
  • Passivating for stainless steel components
  • Zinc and other protective coatings for steel parts
  • Additional treatments available on request, depending on the part

How LeanLine Engineering Works

A simple quoting and supply process that combines overseas machining capacity with local engineering support in NSW.

1. Share Your Drawings or Idea

Send us a drawing, STEP file, sketch or photo with key dimensions, material and any special requirements.

  • We can work from formal drawings or clear sketches
  • Help refining tolerances and critical dimensions
  • Advice on making the part easier to machine

2. We Source, You Deal Locally

We work with selected overseas machining partners to manufacture your parts, and arrange delivery to you.

  • Parts produced by trusted CNC suppliers
  • Surface treatments included in the same quote
  • Some orders ship directly from the supplier to your site

3. Transparent Lead Times & Pricing

Before you commit, we’ll confirm pricing and estimated lead times based on quantity, material and treatments.

  • Quotes that include machining and finishing
  • Clear on minimum order quantities where they apply
  • Updates provided if lead times change

4. Local Support & Follow-Up

If a part doesn’t arrive as expected, you contact LeanLine Engineering directly and we handle it with our suppliers.

  • NSW-based point of contact
  • Support in line with Australian Consumer Law
  • Focus on long-term, repeat customers
